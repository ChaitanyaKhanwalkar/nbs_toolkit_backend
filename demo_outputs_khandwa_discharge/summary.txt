PLAIN-ENGLISH RESULT
--------------------
Against a strong domestic-sewage influent discharged to the Narmada at Khandwa, the
engine ranked all eight trains (none were hard-filtered out). The VF nitrifying hybrid
(train 5) ranked #1 (Ci 0.832, high confidence) because it scores well on treatment-fit
and standard-fit for the high BOD/ammonia/phosphorus load and nitrifies. DEWATS modular
(train 3, Ci 0.693) and the WSP pond series (train 1, Ci 0.607) followed, then Septic+HSSF
polishing (train 4, 0.605), French VF (train 6, 0.583), UASB-based STP (train 2, 0.496),
and Pond + sewage-fed aquaculture (train 8, 0.452). On-site disposal (train 7) sorted
last despite a raw Ci of 0.691, because its discharge use-case suitability is unknown
(no canonical evidence) and three of its criteria were median-imputed; its confidence is
correctly low (0.45).

Nothing was removed before ranking: zero trains rejected, zero components filtered. Most
notably, on-site disposal / soak-pit was NOT hard-filtered on soil even though the site is
low-infiltration clay and a matching hard rule (APP_RULE_023) exists — the soil_infiltration
factor is not wired into train ranking and the value 'low' is never mapped to the rule's
category token, so the filter cannot fire. That is a disclosed gap, not a soil-validated
pass. Missing train-criterion and removal values are treated as disclosed unknowns
(median-imputed for geometry only, flagged, and they lower confidence) — never as 0% removal.
